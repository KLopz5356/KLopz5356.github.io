HOW TO INSTALL RAM IN YOUR PC







Learning how to add RAM is an excellent solution to speed up your computer if you're wanting to do so. Most operating systems are memory hogs, but if you have a recent computer or laptop, you generally won't notice much of a difference in how quickly you can browse the web. However, older computers are a different matter.

Luckily, installing RAM is quite simple because it's a crucial component of your computer. At least when it comes to installing PC components, it doesn't get any easier than this. You should be able to pick it up quite fast even if you have never done it before.





STEP 1: READ YOUR MOTHERBOARD MANUAL

When it comes to installing a RAM, you shouldn't be hasty about jamming it into any available slots on your motherboard, considering how simple it is to pop RAM sticks in there. After all, you want to make sure the RAM is in the right slots so you can get the most performance out of it.

When your computer has many RAM sticks, using it in dual- or quad-channel modes will enhance performance. To do this, you must use the appropriate RAM slots.You may find out which of your slots are connected in this way by consulting your motherboard's instructions, but to make things simpler, they are often all constructed of the same color plastic. The slots indicated in the next step will be used to install dual-channel RAM.


STEP 2: OPEN YOUR RAM SLOTS

Once you've determined where your RAMS should go, you may begin installing now. There will be two small clips on either side of each RAM slot. Press these down to open them. Use moderate power,they don't need to move too far.

You might only need to perform this at one end of your RAMÂ slot in some circumstances. Older form factors, such DDR3, open at both ends, but DDR4 memory frequently only has one tab that may be moved.


STEP 3: LINE UP YOUR RAM

RAM sticks are keyed, which means they have a gap in the connection that ensures you can only enter them one way. Align your RAM such that the gap on the connector matches to the RAM slot.


STEP 4: INSERT THE RAM

When your RAM is properly aligned, slide it into its slot. If your RAM slot only has one open tab, place the RAM first into the end that isn't open. If your DIMM slot has two open tabs, you must install the RAM equally.

Once your RAM is in the slot, press down on both ends of it until you hear/feel a click and see the tab(s) shut to secure it. New motherboards are sometimes fairly rigid, and you may need to apply some pressure to achieve this. If your motherboard flexes or the RAM slot slides, pause and double-check that you did the previous procedures correctly.
